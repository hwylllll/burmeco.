01

What is Fast Fashion?

Fast fashion is expanding in Myanmar, but it brings several challenges. Major brands like H&M and Primark now manufacture there, boosting exports. However, garment workers are often underpaid and lack proper labor protections. At the same time, cheap imported clothing from China and Thailand is harming Myanmar’s traditional textile industry. In response, some local designers are working to preserve these traditions by using handwoven, naturally dyed fabrics.

02

The Perfect Solution

Myanmar is tackling fast fashion by promoting eco-friendly alternatives like eco printing and traditional handwoven textiles. These use natural dyes and local materials to reduce environmental impact. By supporting artisans and encouraging mindful consumption, Myanmar preserves its cultural heritage while addressing environmental challenges and fostering ethical fashion growth.

03

Why it works

Local culture values traditional crafts and handmade goods, encouraging communities to reconnect with meaningful practices. Sustainable fashion skills training benefits young people, while second-hand shopping and clothing swaps promote mindful consumption.
